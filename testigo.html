Testigo en la Polvadera (2024)

[Sound piece, graphic score, cement-based sculptural transfer]. This ongoing practical study applies principles of musical indeterminacy to explore the cognitive link between spatial perception and associative processes.

Using digitally manipulated photographs taken in the localities of Bihać and Golubić (Bosnia-Herzegovina) in 2024, a graphic score is developed. This process contributes to the formation of a contextual language in which the relationship between graphic elements alludes to timbral and textural qualities of sound.

Through a process of abstraction and transfer, individual elements of the graphic score are transformed into a synthetic model reflecting aspects of the geographic context where it was conceived: Historical marks on architecture—wear, graffiti, and bullet holes that remain three decades after the war—combine with symbols drawn from the landscape, and their features are artificially reorganized into a series of tangible objects.

These physical objects, through their materiality and interaction with sound, highlight and spatially dimension specific elements of a perceptual code.

Alongside the graphic score and material objects, the project includes the first sound interpretation, created using field recordings made in Golubić and Jalisco, later processed with digital tools.

This project was developed during the artistic residency Macrobiotopes: New Ecologies of Transcultural Exchanges, organized by the OKRA Foundation (Czech Republic) and the KRAK Center for Contemporary Culture (Bosnia-Herzegovina).

"Testigo en la Polvadera" is a part of the upcoming album "Ciclos de Entumecimiento".
