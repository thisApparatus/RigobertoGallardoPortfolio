About

Rigoberto Gallardo is a Jalisco-born, Prague-based interdisciplinary artist and musician. His musical practice (under the alias mvd0ae) explores the cultural entanglements evoked by sonic materials, manifesting dissonance through fractured rhythms that contrast stillness with turmoil.

As a sculptor, he uses diverse elements to examine contradic- tions in interpersonal relationships and larger social phenomena, highlighting their complexity and interconnectedness.

Drawing from personal and collective experiences, Gallardo explores how societal constructs and human behaviors interact across cultural and physical realms. His work emphasizes these interconnections, inviting reflection through sound, light, and tangible materials.

A skepticism toward normative constructs drives his curiosity to question and deconstruct them, focusing on faith, tradition, social movements, and how historical events challenge established norms.

Using both symbolic and synthetic elements, he fosters dialogue about contemporary issues within a historical context, embracing diverse perspectives.

Identity and its construction-deconstruction are central to his practice. His mixed ethnicity and bicultural upbringing (Latinoamérica-Western Europe) lead him to examine how people perceive themselves and the values shaped by memory, culture, and geography. Migration, cultural friction, gender, and globalization are key themes in his work, reflecting both his personal experience and active participation in these issues.
